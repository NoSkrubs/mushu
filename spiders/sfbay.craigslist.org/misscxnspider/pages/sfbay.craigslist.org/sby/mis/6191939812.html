\nI was communicating with a therapist who helps people with BPD, PTSD, abuse issues etc and after telling her a little about our situation ( I realize it is no longer our anything) and she quickly proffered an insight that I found both valid and damning, and it made me realize I have not apologized for something I meant to from almost the moment my words left my mouth. First her insight. She said that it was likely something I did or said triggered you to start making plans to leave, probably unbeknownst to me. THe moment I read the words I knew what it was and I was mortified because I never addressed it, though I felt horrible for saying it, I never brought it up again even to apologize because of the nature of the issue. I am going to try and do this without offering up too much in the way of detail. I told you that you were culpable for something that happened because you were aware of it happening once and had you moved to force responsibility on those parties involved it would not have happened the second time. This was unfair, and I was lashing out because of the halo you seemed to be placing over one party who I felt was totally undeserving of your love or adoration. Instead of using the statement to shut down your feelings or at least your discussion about your feelings for this person I should have stopped myself and remembered that I love you and what I said was deeply hurtful. I don\'t think anything I did or said past that point mattered in your eyes. I am truly sorry, and when I look at that point in time it does seem to be where things began to change. You put on a good show of it but I think perhaps after that you were out. I wish we could have talked about it at a later point but I think it was too hurtful to you and I was too mortified at what I said for either of us to discuss it again. I am sorry. I would want you to know that was not in my head from that point on. That is not how I saw you, nor did it have any impact on my deep feelings for you. I just wish I would have realized what a poison pill it was because I just fell deeper in love with you while you made plans to leave. I would liken it to fishing as a kid and not realizing that if you let the fish play with the bait and hook too long often it swallows the hook getting it caught down in its esophagus and not in the lip making it almost impossible to return it to the water in any state where it might live. I feel like that fish, but if it is of my own doing than I have to take my lumps. I just wish we would have made some sort of peace before everything blew up.
\nI miss you, I still love you, and if this is in any way true I guess I don\'t ever expect to hear from you, but that doesn\'t mean I give up the hope you will. Peace which means so much more to me than when you described it that one time. It is a symbol of one of the happiest days of my life...
