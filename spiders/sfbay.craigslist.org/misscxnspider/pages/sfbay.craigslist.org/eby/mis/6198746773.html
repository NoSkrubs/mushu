\nWe spent the last day of Middle School together. It was the graduation field trip to Great America. My group of friends met up with your group of friends and we walked around together. I even won you a giant green stuffed animal snake. I wrote you a special message on your arm right before we got on the bus. Not to sound too dorky but it is still one of the happiest days of my life.
\n
\nAfter that we went to different High Schools and never saw each other again. I sent you a written letter once through a mutual friend. It spoke of your beautiful smile that lights up the world and melts hearts. Especially mine. I also saw you once at Straw Hat but I was too nervous to talk to you. My heart was jumping out of my chest and you came over to say hi to me. I said hi but I couldn\'t look at you because it hurt to see something so pretty.
\n
\nYou\'ve probably forgotten about me but I\'ve never ever forgotten about you. I heard you are married now and have kids. And your husband has the same name as me. I too had kids with a woman who happened to have the SAME NAME AS YOU. How insane is that? I used to think of as a sign that you and I were supposed to be with each other. Lol. Hella corny I know.
\n
\nAnyways I\'ve been thinking a whole bunch about you. Looked you up on social media but no luck. So this is my attempt to contact you. I doubt it\'ll ever reach your eyes though. Your big gorgeous round eyes underneath those thick beautiful dark eyebrows. You were the prettiest girl that I ever knew Amanda. I wish I would\'ve had more time with you.
\n
\nP.S. If you somehow do actually read this then you probably know who I am by now. But just in case here are a few more hints:

\n-I bought you a ring with your birth stone on it. It\'s orange. Which you told me was your favorite color

\n-We had PE together and you always stood by the wall looking super cute so I would come talk to you.
